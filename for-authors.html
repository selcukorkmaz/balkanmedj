Author Guidelines

Thank you for considering Balkan Medical Journal for your manuscript submission. Please review these guidelines carefully before preparing your manuscript. Adherence to these instructions will ensure a smooth submission and review process.

Balkan Medical Journal accepts the following manuscript types. Please ensure your submission conforms to the word limits, reference counts, and figure/table allowances specified below.

Type Word Limit Tables/Figures References
Original Article 4,000 6 50
Review 5,000 8 100
Brief Report 2,000 3 20
Case Report 2,000 3 20
Letter to the Editor 1,000 1 10
Image Corner 500 3 images 5
Editorial 2,000 2 20
  • Original Article: Reports of original clinical or experimental research. Maximum 4,000 words (excluding abstract, references, tables, and figure legends), up to 6 tables/figures combined, and 50 references.
  • Review: Comprehensive reviews of a specific topic. May be invited or submitted. Maximum 5,000 words with up to 100 references. Systematic reviews and meta-analyses are strongly encouraged.
  • Brief Report: Concise reports of preliminary or novel findings. Maximum 2,000 words, 3 tables/figures, and 20 references.
  • Case Report: Descriptions of unusual or instructive clinical cases. Maximum 2,000 words, up to 3 figures, and 20 references. Must include a clear take-home message.
  • Letter to the Editor: Comments on recently published articles or brief observations. Maximum 1,000 words and 10 references.
  • Image Corner: Striking clinical or diagnostic images with a brief explanation. Maximum 500 words, up to 3 images, and 5 references.
  • Editorial: Invited commentaries on topics of current interest or on articles published in the journal. By invitation only.

Title Page

The title page must be submitted as a separate file and should include:

  • Full title of the manuscript (concise and informative)
  • A short running title (no more than 50 characters)
  • Full names, academic degrees, ORCID identifiers, and institutional affiliations of all authors
  • Name, address, phone, fax, and e-mail of the corresponding author
  • Acknowledgments, funding sources, and conflict of interest disclosures
  • Word count for the abstract and main text separately

Abstract

For Original Articles, the abstract must be structured with the following headings:

  • Objective: State the primary aim of the study.
  • Methods: Describe the study design, setting, participants, interventions, and main outcome measures.
  • Results: Present the main findings with specific data and statistical significance.
  • Conclusion: State the primary conclusions and their clinical implications.

The abstract should not exceed 250 words. Reviews, case reports, and brief reports should include unstructured abstracts of no more than 200 words.

Keywords

Provide 3 to 6 keywords. Keywords should be selected from the Medical Subject Headings (MeSH) list of the U.S. National Library of Medicine (meshb.nlm.nih.gov).

Text Formatting

  • Manuscripts should be prepared in Microsoft Word format (.doc or .docx).
  • Use 12-point Times New Roman font, double-spaced throughout.
  • Number all pages consecutively, starting with the title page.
  • Use SI (International System) units for measurements.
  • Abbreviations should be defined at first mention in the abstract and the main text separately.

References

References should be formatted according to the Vancouver style (Uniform Requirements for Manuscripts Submitted to Biomedical Journals). Number references consecutively in the order in which they first appear in the text. Identify references in the text with superscript Arabic numerals. Example:

1. Sahin A, Yilmaz B, Ozdemir C. Effect of treatment on clinical outcomes in patients with diabetes. Balkan Med J. 2024;41(2):123-130.

Tables

Each table should be on a separate page, numbered consecutively with Arabic numerals, and have a descriptive title. Do not use vertical lines. Explain all abbreviations in footnotes.

Figures

  • Figures must be submitted as separate files in TIFF or JPEG format.
  • Minimum resolution: 300 dpi for all figures.
  • Color figures should be in CMYK mode for print.
  • Each figure must have a legend/caption that makes it comprehensible without reference to the text.
  • Patients shown in photographs must have their identity concealed, or written consent for publication must be obtained.

All manuscripts must be submitted online through the journal's manuscript management system:

balkanmedj.manuscriptmanager.net

You will need to create an account if you are a first-time user.

Required Files

The following files must be uploaded during submission:

  1. Cover Letter: Address to the Editor-in-Chief. State the manuscript type, confirm originality, and disclose any prior or dual publication or submission.
  2. Title Page: A separate document containing all author information, affiliations, ORCID IDs, and corresponding author details.
  3. Main Document: The blinded manuscript file containing the abstract, keywords, main text, references, table titles, and figure legends. Do not include any author-identifying information.
  4. Figures: Each figure as a separate high-resolution file (TIFF or JPEG, minimum 300 dpi).
  5. Tables: Included at the end of the main document or as separate files.
  6. Supplementary Material: Any additional data, appendices, or multimedia files that support the manuscript (optional).

ORCID Requirement

All authors must provide their ORCID (Open Researcher and Contributor ID) at the time of submission. If you do not have an ORCID, you can register for free at orcid.org. ORCID identifiers will be published alongside author names in the final article.

Important: Manuscripts that do not comply with the submission guidelines will be returned to the authors before being sent for peer review. Please ensure all required files are included and properly formatted.

Ethics Committee Approval

All studies involving human participants or animal subjects must have ethics committee approval. The approval number and the name of the ethics committee must be stated in the Methods section of the manuscript. Balkan Medical Journal adheres to the principles of the Declaration of Helsinki.

Informed Consent

Written informed consent must be obtained from all participants (or their legal guardians) in studies involving human subjects. For case reports and image submissions, a signed patient consent form must be submitted. Patient confidentiality must be maintained at all times.

ICMJE Disclosure Forms

Each author must complete an ICMJE Conflict of Interest Disclosure Form. These forms should be uploaded during the submission process. The ICMJE form is available for download on our Forms page.

Clinical Trial Registration

All clinical trials must be registered in a public trials registry (e.g., ClinicalTrials.gov, WHO International Clinical Trials Registry Platform) before enrollment of the first participant. The registration number must be provided in the abstract and Methods section.

Reporting Guidelines

Authors are required to follow the appropriate reporting guidelines for their study design. Completed checklists must be submitted with the manuscript:

  • CONSORT -- for randomized controlled trials
  • STROBE -- for observational studies
  • PRISMA -- for systematic reviews and meta-analyses
  • STARD -- for diagnostic accuracy studies
  • CARE -- for case reports
  • ARRIVE -- for animal studies

For a complete list of reporting guidelines, visit the EQUATOR Network.

Double-Blind Review

Balkan Medical Journal employs a double-blind peer review process. Neither the authors nor the reviewers are aware of each other's identities during the evaluation. This ensures objectivity and fairness in the review process.

Typical Timeline

1
Initial Screening: 3-5 business days after submission
2
Peer Review: 4-6 weeks for the initial decision
3
Revision Period: Authors are typically given 30 days for major revisions, 14 days for minor revisions
4
Final Decision: Usually within 2 weeks after revised manuscript submission

Revision Requirements

When revisions are requested, authors must submit a detailed point-by-point response to each reviewer comment. Changes in the manuscript should be highlighted or tracked. Failure to address reviewer comments adequately may result in rejection.

Appeal Process

Authors who believe their manuscript was incorrectly rejected may submit a written appeal to the Editor-in-Chief within 30 days of the decision. The appeal should provide a detailed, evidence-based rebuttal of the reviewers' concerns. Appeal decisions are final.

Copyediting

After acceptance, manuscripts undergo professional copyediting for grammar, style, and consistency. Authors may be contacted for clarification during this process. The copyedited version will be sent to the corresponding author for approval.

Proof Review

Page proofs will be sent to the corresponding author in PDF format. Authors must review proofs carefully and return corrections within 48 hours. Only minor corrections and typographical errors may be corrected at the proof stage. Substantial changes to the content are not permitted.

Online-First Publication

Accepted articles are published online ahead of print as "Articles in Press" on the journal website. Online-first articles are citable using their DOI and are fully indexed. This allows rapid dissemination of research findings before the formal issue publication.

DOI Assignment

Each article is assigned a unique Digital Object Identifier (DOI) upon online-first publication. The DOI provides a permanent link to the article and should be used for citation purposes. DOIs are registered with CrossRef and are indexed across all major databases.
