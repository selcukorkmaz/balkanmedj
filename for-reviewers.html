Reviewer Guidelines

Peer reviewers are the cornerstone of scholarly publishing. We sincerely thank our reviewers for their invaluable contributions to maintaining and elevating the scientific quality of the Balkan Medical Journal. Your expertise, time, and dedication are essential to the integrity of the peer review process.

Balkan Medical Journal employs a rigorous double-blind peer review process, ensuring that neither the authors nor the reviewers are aware of each other's identities throughout the evaluation. This approach promotes impartiality and objectivity in the assessment of all submitted manuscripts.

  • Manuscripts are assigned to reviewers by the handling editors based on the reviewer's area of expertise and the subject matter of the submission.
  • Reviewers are expected to complete their evaluation within 21 days of accepting the review invitation. If additional time is needed, reviewers should notify the editorial office promptly.
  • All communication between reviewers and the editorial office is conducted through the Manuscript Manager online submission and review system.
  • Reviewers will receive an email invitation with a link to access the manuscript and submit their review directly through the system.

If a reviewer is unable to complete the review within the given timeframe, they are encouraged to decline the invitation as early as possible so that an alternative reviewer can be assigned without unnecessary delays.

Reviewers play a critical role in upholding the standards of the journal. By accepting a review invitation, reviewers agree to the following responsibilities:

  • Maintain strict confidentiality: The manuscript and all associated materials must be treated as confidential documents. Reviewers must not share, discuss, or disclose the content of the manuscript with anyone outside the review process.
  • Disclose conflicts of interest: If a reviewer identifies any potential conflict of interest -- whether financial, institutional, personal, or intellectual -- they must promptly inform the editor and, if necessary, recuse themselves from the review.
  • Provide constructive and objective feedback: Reviews should be thorough, fair, and focused on improving the quality of the manuscript. Personal criticism of the authors is unacceptable; feedback should address the science, methodology, and presentation.
  • Meet agreed-upon deadlines: Timely reviews are essential to the efficiency of the editorial process. Reviewers should complete their evaluation within the 21-day review period or communicate any delays to the editorial office.
  • Decline if outside expertise area: If a manuscript falls outside the reviewer's area of competence, they should decline the invitation promptly and, where possible, suggest alternative qualified reviewers.

When evaluating a manuscript, reviewers should consider the following criteria to ensure a comprehensive and balanced assessment:

Originality

Does the study provide novel findings or a fresh perspective on an existing topic?

Scientific Validity

Are the conclusions supported by the data and evidence presented?

Methodology

Is the study design appropriate and clearly described? Are the methods reproducible?

Statistical Analysis

Are the statistical methods appropriate and correctly applied?

Clinical Relevance

Does the work have practical significance for clinicians or researchers?

Writing Quality

Is the manuscript clearly written with logical organization and appropriate language?

Ethical Compliance

Does the study meet ethical standards including informed consent and IRB approval?

References & Figures

Are references adequate and current? Are tables and figures clear and necessary?

A well-structured review helps authors understand the strengths and weaknesses of their manuscript and provides clear guidance for revision. We recommend organizing your review as follows:

  1. Summary of the Manuscript

    Provide a brief overview of the study's objectives, methods, and key findings. This demonstrates your understanding of the work and helps editors assess the review.

  2. Major Comments (numbered)

    Address significant issues that affect the validity, interpretation, or impact of the study. These may include fundamental methodological flaws, insufficient data, unsupported conclusions, or major gaps in the literature review.

  3. Minor Comments (numbered)

    Note smaller issues such as typographical errors, unclear phrasing, formatting inconsistencies, minor data presentation issues, or suggestions for additional references.

  4. Recommendation

    Provide your overall recommendation for the manuscript. Choose from:

    • Accept -- The manuscript is suitable for publication in its current form.
    • Minor Revision -- Small changes are needed before acceptance.
    • Major Revision -- Substantial revisions are required; the manuscript will be re-reviewed.
    • Reject -- The manuscript is not suitable for the journal.
  5. Confidential Comments to Editors

    Use this section to communicate any concerns to the editors that should not be shared with the authors, such as suspected ethical issues, conflicts of interest, or additional context for your recommendation.

Ethical integrity is paramount in the peer review process. Reviewers are expected to adhere to the highest ethical standards, consistent with the guidelines established by the Committee on Publication Ethics (COPE).

  • Report suspected plagiarism or misconduct: If you suspect that a manuscript contains plagiarized material, fabricated or falsified data, or any other form of research misconduct, you must report your concerns to the editor immediately through the confidential comments section.
  • Maintain objectivity: Reviews should be based solely on the scientific merit of the work. Personal, political, religious, or other non-scientific considerations should not influence the evaluation.
  • Avoid using unpublished data: Reviewers must not use any ideas, data, or information obtained through the peer review process for their own research or any other purpose without the explicit written consent of the authors.
  • COPE guidelines compliance: Balkan Medical Journal follows COPE guidelines for peer reviewers. All reviewers are encouraged to familiarize themselves with these ethical guidelines to ensure best practices in peer review.

Balkan Medical Journal values the contributions of its reviewers and is committed to recognizing their essential role in the publication process. We offer several forms of acknowledgment and recognition:

Annual Reviewer Acknowledgment

The names of all reviewers who have contributed during the year are published in the last issue of each volume as a formal acknowledgment of their service.

Reviewer Certificates

Reviewers may request an official certificate confirming their contribution to the peer review process, which can be used for academic portfolios and promotion applications.

Publons / Web of Science Recognition

Reviewers can claim their review activity on Publons (Web of Science Reviewer Recognition Service) to build a verified record of their peer review contributions.

Annual Outstanding Reviewer Award

Each year, the editorial board selects outstanding reviewers based on the quality, thoroughness, and timeliness of their reviews. Award recipients are recognized in the journal and receive a certificate of excellence.
