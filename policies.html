Journal Policies

Balkan Medical Journal adheres to the highest standards of publication ethics and transparency. Our policies are designed to ensure integrity, fairness, and openness at every stage of the publication process.

Open Access Policy

Balkan Medical Journal operates under a Diamond Open Access model. This means that all published content is freely available to readers worldwide immediately upon publication, without any embargo period. Unlike Gold Open Access journals, we do not charge article processing charges (APCs) to authors.

No Fees at Any Stage

  • No submission fees -- Authors are never charged for submitting a manuscript for consideration.
  • No article processing charges (APCs) -- Accepted articles are published at no cost to authors.
  • No subscription fees -- Readers can access all articles freely without subscriptions or paywalls.
  • No page charges -- There are no fees based on article length, number of figures, or color illustrations.

Funding and Sustainability

The journal is funded entirely by Trakya University, Edirne, Türkiye, which covers all costs of editorial operations, peer review management, copyediting, typesetting, hosting, and archiving. This institutional support ensures that financial barriers do not prevent researchers from publishing or accessing scientific knowledge.

Compliance and Alignment

  • Plan S compliant -- Our open access model fully satisfies the requirements of Plan S and cOAlition S funders.
  • Budapest Open Access Initiative (BOAI) -- We are aligned with the principles of BOAI, supporting free and unrestricted access to scholarly research.
  • Directory of Open Access Journals (DOAJ) -- Balkan Medical Journal is indexed in DOAJ, confirming our adherence to best practices in open access publishing.

Immediate Access

All articles are available in full text (HTML and PDF) immediately upon publication. There are no registration walls, login requirements, or restricted access periods. We believe that removing barriers to access accelerates scientific discovery and promotes equitable participation in research.

Publication Ethics

Balkan Medical Journal is committed to upholding the highest standards of publication ethics. We follow the guidelines and recommendations of the Committee on Publication Ethics (COPE) and expect all authors, reviewers, and editors to adhere to these principles.

COPE Membership

The journal is a member of COPE (Committee on Publication Ethics). All ethical concerns and suspected misconduct cases are handled in accordance with COPE flowcharts and guidelines. Our editors regularly participate in COPE seminars and training to stay current with best practices.

Plagiarism Screening

All submitted manuscripts are screened for plagiarism and text similarity using iThenticate software before being sent for peer review. Manuscripts with unacceptable levels of similarity may be returned to authors for revision or rejected outright. Self-plagiarism and redundant publication are also checked and addressed.

Duplicate and Simultaneous Submission

Manuscripts submitted to Balkan Medical Journal must not be under simultaneous consideration by any other publication. Submitting the same manuscript to multiple journals concurrently is a serious breach of publication ethics. Authors must confirm at the time of submission that the work is not being considered elsewhere.

Authorship Criteria

Balkan Medical Journal follows the International Committee of Medical Journal Editors (ICMJE) recommendations for authorship. All listed authors must meet all four of the following criteria:

  • Substantial contributions to the conception or design of the work, or the acquisition, analysis, or interpretation of data.
  • Drafting the work or revising it critically for important intellectual content.
  • Final approval of the version to be published.
  • Agreement to be accountable for all aspects of the work, ensuring that questions related to accuracy or integrity are appropriately investigated and resolved.

Gift authorship, ghost authorship, and honorary authorship are not permitted. Changes to the author list after submission require written consent from all authors and a detailed explanation.

Conflicts of Interest

All authors are required to disclose any financial or non-financial conflicts of interest that could be perceived to influence the research or its interpretation. Reviewers and editors must also declare any conflicts and recuse themselves from handling manuscripts where a conflict exists. Conflict of interest statements are published alongside accepted articles.

Corrections and Retractions

When errors or ethical issues are identified after publication, the journal follows COPE guidelines for issuing corrections (errata/corrigenda), expressions of concern, or retractions as appropriate. See the Corrections & Retractions tab for full details.

Research Involving Human Subjects

All research involving human participants must have been conducted in accordance with the Declaration of Helsinki and must have received approval from an appropriate institutional review board (IRB) or ethics committee. Informed consent must be obtained from all participants, and this must be stated in the manuscript. When reporting studies involving vulnerable populations, additional safeguards must be documented.

Animal Research Ethics

Studies involving animals must comply with institutional and national guidelines for the care and use of laboratory animals. Authors must provide details of the ethical approval obtained and confirm that the study followed the principles of the 3Rs (Replacement, Reduction, and Refinement). The institutional review board or ethics committee approval number must be included in the manuscript.

Copyright & Licensing

Balkan Medical Journal publishes all articles under the Creative Commons Attribution-NonCommercial-NoDerivatives 4.0 International (CC BY-NC-ND 4.0) license. This license ensures broad access while protecting the integrity of the original work.

Authors Retain Copyright

Upon acceptance, authors retain the copyright to their published work. Balkan Medical Journal does not require authors to transfer copyright ownership to the journal or publisher. Instead, authors grant the journal a non-exclusive license to publish the article. This means authors remain free to use their own work for educational, institutional, and personal purposes.

CC BY-NC-ND 4.0 License Terms

Under the CC BY-NC-ND 4.0 license, readers and third parties are free to:

  • Share -- Copy and redistribute the material in any medium or format for non-commercial purposes.
  • Attribution -- Appropriate credit must be given to the original authors and the journal, with a link to the license and an indication of any changes made.

Under this license, the following restrictions apply:

  • NonCommercial -- The material may not be used for commercial purposes without explicit permission from the copyright holder.
  • NoDerivatives -- If others remix, transform, or build upon the material, they may not distribute the modified material without permission. Translations and adaptations require the consent of the original authors.

Copyright Transfer Form

Upon acceptance of a manuscript, all authors are required to complete and sign a Copyright Transfer Form. This form confirms that the work is original, has not been published elsewhere, and that all authors agree to the terms of the CC BY-NC-ND 4.0 license. The form must be submitted before the article can proceed to production. The copyright transfer form is available on our Forms page.

Reuse and Permissions

For uses beyond those permitted by the CC BY-NC-ND 4.0 license (such as commercial reuse, inclusion in derivative works, or republication in modified form), please contact the corresponding author of the article or the journal editorial office at info@balkanmedicaljournal.org. All permissions requests will be considered on a case-by-case basis.

Data Sharing Policy

Balkan Medical Journal encourages transparency and reproducibility in research by promoting the sharing of data, materials, and code underlying published articles. We believe that open data strengthens the scientific record and facilitates future research.

Data Availability Statement

All submitted manuscripts must include a Data Availability Statement indicating whether and how the data supporting the findings can be accessed. This statement should specify the repository where data are deposited, any restrictions on access, or a justification if data cannot be shared (e.g., due to privacy or ethical constraints).

Supplementary Materials

The journal accepts supplementary materials such as additional tables, figures, datasets, videos, and appendices that support the main findings of an article. Supplementary materials are peer-reviewed alongside the main manuscript and are published online with persistent links to the parent article.

Data Repositories

Authors are encouraged to deposit their research data in recognized, discipline-appropriate public repositories. Recommended repositories include but are not limited to:

  • Figshare -- General-purpose research data repository.
  • Dryad -- Curated digital repository for research data.
  • Zenodo -- Open-access repository operated by CERN.
  • GenBank / NCBI -- For genomic and sequence data.
  • ClinicalTrials.gov -- For clinical trial datasets and registrations.

When data are deposited in a repository, the dataset DOI or accession number should be included in the Data Availability Statement and, where appropriate, cited in the reference list.

Clinical Trial Data Sharing

In accordance with the ICMJE recommendations, authors of clinical trials are strongly encouraged to share de-identified individual participant data (IPD) and related documents (such as study protocols and statistical analysis plans). The journal supports the ICMJE data sharing statement requirements and asks authors to include a data sharing plan at the time of trial registration and manuscript submission.

Exceptions and Restrictions

We recognize that data sharing may not always be possible due to ethical, legal, or privacy considerations. In such cases, authors must provide a clear explanation in the Data Availability Statement. Restricted-access data (available upon reasonable request) and data governed by institutional data use agreements are acceptable, provided the conditions of access are clearly documented.

Corrections & Retractions

Balkan Medical Journal is committed to maintaining the accuracy and integrity of the published scientific record. When errors or ethical issues are identified after publication, we take appropriate corrective action in accordance with COPE (Committee on Publication Ethics) guidelines.

Erratum (Publisher Correction)

An erratum is issued when a significant error introduced during the production process (e.g., typesetting, formatting, or copy-editing) has affected the article. The erratum notice clearly identifies the error and provides the correct information. The original article is updated with a link to the erratum, and the erratum is published as a separate, citable notice in the journal.

Corrigendum (Author Correction)

A corrigendum is issued when the authors identify a significant error in their published work that affects the interpretation or reproducibility of the findings but does not invalidate the overall conclusions. The corrigendum notice describes the error and provides the corrected information. Authors are encouraged to promptly notify the journal of any errors they discover after publication.

Retraction

A retraction is issued when the findings of an article are determined to be unreliable or fundamentally flawed, or when serious ethical violations are confirmed. Retractions are handled in accordance with COPE retraction guidelines. Grounds for retraction include:

  • Clear evidence of unreliable findings due to misconduct (e.g., data fabrication or falsification) or honest error.
  • Plagiarism or substantial overlap with a previously published work.
  • Failure to disclose a major competing interest that would have influenced the editorial decision.
  • Use of data or images without proper authorization or ethical approval.
  • Copyright infringement or other serious legal issues.

Retracted articles are not removed from the journal archives. Instead, a retraction notice is published and linked to the original article, which is watermarked to indicate its retracted status. The retraction notice explains the reason for retraction and is freely accessible.

Expression of Concern

An expression of concern may be issued when there is significant evidence of potential problems with a published article, but the investigation is ongoing or inconclusive. This notice alerts readers that the reliability of the findings is in question. Once the investigation is concluded, the expression of concern may be followed by a retraction, correction, or resolution notice.

Article Removal

In exceptional circumstances, an article may be removed from the online publication. This action is reserved for cases where the article contains content that poses a serious health risk if acted upon, where the article clearly violates personal privacy or constitutes defamation, or where the article is the subject of a court order. In such cases, the metadata (title and authors) is retained along with a notice explaining the reason for removal, but the full-text content is no longer available.

Privacy Policy

Balkan Medical Journal is committed to protecting the privacy and personal data of all users, including authors, reviewers, editors, and readers. This policy outlines how we collect, use, store, and protect personal information.

Data Collection

Personal data is collected through our online manuscript submission and review system. The types of data collected may include:

  • Name, institutional affiliation, and contact details (email address, postal address, phone number).
  • ORCID iD and other academic identifiers.
  • Manuscript files, cover letters, and related correspondence.
  • Reviewer reports and editorial decision records.
  • Website usage data collected via analytics and cookies.

GDPR Compliance

Balkan Medical Journal complies with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) of the European Union. We process personal data lawfully, fairly, and transparently. Users have the right to access, correct, delete, or restrict the processing of their personal data. To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at info@balkanmedicaljournal.org.

Use of Email Addresses

Email addresses provided during registration or submission are used exclusively for journal-related communication. This includes editorial correspondence, peer review notifications, production updates, and, where consented to, newsletter and journal alert emails. Email addresses are never sold or shared with third parties for marketing purposes. Users may unsubscribe from non-essential communications at any time.

Third-Party Sharing

Personal data is not shared with third parties except where necessary for journal operations (e.g., our publisher Galenos Publishing House, the manuscript management system provider, and indexing/archiving services). All third-party service providers are bound by data protection agreements consistent with GDPR requirements. We do not sell, rent, or trade personal information.

Cookie Policy

The Balkan Medical Journal website uses cookies to enhance user experience and analyze site traffic. The types of cookies used include:

  • Essential cookies -- Required for the website and submission system to function properly. These cannot be disabled.
  • Analytics cookies -- Used to understand how visitors interact with the website (e.g., page views, session duration). These are anonymized and aggregated.
  • Preference cookies -- Store user preferences such as language settings and display options.

Users can manage cookie preferences through their browser settings. Disabling certain cookies may affect the functionality of the website.

Data Retention and Security

Personal data is retained only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected, or as required by legal obligations. We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect personal data against unauthorized access, loss, destruction, or alteration. This includes encrypted connections (HTTPS), access controls, and regular security reviews of our systems.
